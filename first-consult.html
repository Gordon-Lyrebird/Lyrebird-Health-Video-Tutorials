How to Do Your First Consult and Referral Letter with Lyrebird

Step-by-Step Instructions

Overview: This tutorial demonstrates how to create your Lyrebird account within Best Practice, conduct your first consultation with automated transcription, and generate specialist referral letters directly from the consultation notes. Learn how to use Lyrebird without touching your keyboard during patient interactions.
  1. Open a patient's record in Best Practice and click the L icon at the top of the screen to launch Lyrebird within the patient file.
  2. If this is your first time using Lyrebird, you'll be prompted to create your account. Follow the account creation steps. If you've already logged in for the day, you'll see the blue Lyrebird screen.
  3. Obtain patient consent for recording, then Lyrebird will automatically begin recording the conversation when you see the blue screen.
  4. Conduct your consultation naturally. Have a normal conversation with your patient—ask all your questions and discuss their concerns without typing anything. This allows the patient to feel listened to and understood. For example, discuss:
    • Current conditions and symptoms
    • Recent test results
    • Treatment plans
    • Referrals needed
  5. When you mention any observations out loud during the consultation, they will be automatically captured. This includes:
    • Blood pressure
    • Height and weight
    • Temperature
    • Blood sugar levels (BSL)
    • Any other vital signs
  6. When you've finished the consultation, click Stop Listening. Within a few seconds, your consultation notes will be recorded and displayed.
  7. Review the consultation notes for accuracy. This should only take about 30 seconds. If everything looks correct, you can save it directly to the patient file, or make any necessary edits first.
  8. To create referral letters from the same consultation, click Write Document.
  9. Select the type of document you want to create:
    • Letter to specialist (e.g., podiatrist, physiotherapist)
    • Medical certificates (e.g., Centrelink medical certificate)
    • Other referral documents
  10. Within seconds, Lyrebird will generate letters that contain exactly what was discussed in your consultation transcript. Each letter is tailored to the specific recipient.
  11. Copy the generated content and paste it into your Best Practice template. Your BP template should already have your headers, recipients, and signature—you're simply pasting in the reason for referral content.
  12. For fillable PDF forms (like medical certificates), Lyrebird will automatically populate all fields for you based on the consultation.
  13. Click Save to BP for each document you want to save. Medical certificates and other documents will be saved to the "Correspondence Out" section.
  14. Before saving consultation notes, review all vital observations that were captured during your discussion. Lyrebird will display them for verification.
  15. Once you confirm the vital signs are correct, click Save to BP to save the consultation notes to the patient's file.
  16. Verify everything was saved correctly by checking:
    • Consultation notes appear in the patient file
    • All vital observations are captured in the Observations section
    • Documents appear in Correspondence Out, ready to be sent
Key Benefits of This Workflow:
  • No keyboard needed during patient consultations
  • Natural conversation flow maintains patient engagement
  • Automatic capture of vital signs when spoken aloud
  • Generate multiple referral letters from a single consultation
  • All documentation automatically integrated with Best Practice
Pro Tip: Don't change the way you talk during consultations. Lyrebird is designed to capture natural conversation. Simply speak normally with your patient, mention observations as you would naturally, and let Lyrebird handle the documentation in the background.
What Gets Automatically Captured:
  • Complete consultation conversation and clinical discussion
  • All vital observations mentioned during the consultation
  • Patient symptoms and concerns
  • Treatment plans and clinical decisions
  • Referral requirements and reasons
  • Follow-up instructions
Additional Features Available: Beyond basic consultations and referral letters, Lyrebird also supports care plans, health assessments, and many more features. This tutorial shows you the essential workflow you can use immediately after signing up.
